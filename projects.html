Student Dropout Risk Prediction (E-Learning Platform)

Objective: Built a machine learning model to predict student dropout risk in digital courses to enable proactive engagement.

Dataset: 200 students, including demographics, engagement metrics, and dropout status.

EDA Highlights:

  • Higher dropout in Python courses and among female students.
  • Dropout strongly linked to low session count, shorter session duration, and low satisfaction ratings.

Modeling:

  • Preprocessed data (encoding, scaling, feature selection).
  • Tested Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, XGBoost, SVM with 5-fold CV.
  • Best Model: Random Forest ‚Üí F1: 0.82, Accuracy: 82.5%, ROC-AUC: 0.84.

Key Features:

  • session_count (38%)
  • satisfaction_rating (21.8%)
  • avg_session_duration (12.3%)

Business Impact:

  • Early-warning system to identify at-risk students.
  • Targeted interventions: boost engagement, improve satisfaction, support Python learners, and optimize session structures.
  • Potential to reduce dropout by 15‚Äì20%.
